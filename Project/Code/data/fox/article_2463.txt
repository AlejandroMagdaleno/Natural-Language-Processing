Leaders from top civil rights groups visited the 
White House
 Thursday to urge the administration to work with Congress and pass 
voting
 legislation. 
National Action Network president Reverend Al Sharpton said the group of eight strategized with President Biden and Vice President Kamala Harris on a plan to counter GOP efforts to tighten restrictions on voting.

&#34;If we don’t put the street heat on it won’t happen,&#34; Sharpton told reporters following the meeting. &#34;We informed them this is going to come not from the White House down but from our houses up.&#34;
The civil rights groups said Congress needs to pass the &#34;For the People Act&#34; and the &#34;John Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act&#34; to counter what they believe is a targeted attack against minority voters.
The leader of the National Urban League, Marc Morial, accused Republicans of a concerted effort to &#34;suppress&#34; the vote for &#34;black people, brown people, young people, people who are disabled and many other Americans who live with great disadvantage in this country.&#34;
Republicans deny that they are trying to suppress the Black vote and say that restrictions on certain voting practices and imposing identification requirements prevents fraud.
The White House meeting comes one week after the Supreme Court ruled to uphold new voting regulations in Arizona.
