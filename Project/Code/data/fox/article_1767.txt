Department of Homeland Security Secretary Alejandro Mayorkas ordered immigration agencies to take immediate steps to ensure eligible veterans are able to return to the U.S., according to a memo obtained by Fox News. Mayorkas also said the government would review its policies.
&#34;The Department of Homeland Security recognizes the profound commitment and sacrifice that service members and their families have made to the United States of America,&#34; Mayorkas said in a statement. &#34;Together with our partner the Department of Veterans Affairs, we are committed to bringing back military service members, veterans, and their immediate family members who were unjustly removed and ensuring they receive the benefits to which they may be entitled. Today we are taking important steps to make that a reality.&#34; 
DHS is working to set up a resource center to help interested veterans with their immigration applications. It is also working to ensure that veterans have access to the benefits that they are entitled to, in addition to COVID-19 vaccines.
The actions were first reported by The Washington Post on Friday.
