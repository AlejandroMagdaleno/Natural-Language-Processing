Karl Heimer, pastor and CEO at Ysleta Lutheran Mission Human Care, explains how the migrant children that he encounters are doing.
New images show the extent to which the 
migrant facility in Donna, Texas
 has expanded in recent weeks as authorities scramble to find space to house an overwhelming surge of underage people coming across the border. 
The Donna facility has led to some of the most 
iconic images
 so far of the crisis, where reporters and lawmakers have taken pictures of migrants packed together in overcrowded facilities, as authorities have struggled to process them.
INSIDE THE BORDER CRISIS: PRESS TOURS PACKED TEXAS FACILITY WITH 4,000+ MIGRANTS 

      The Donna facility as of April 14, 2021. (Fox News)
      
More than 4,000 migrants in custody were seen packed in &#34;pods&#34;; each pod contained 500-600 migrants. Guidelines say there should be no more than 32 people in each pod.
Fox News’ Flight Team drone took photos of the Donna facility in March and on Wednesday, showing that the facility has expanded in recent weeks. 

      The Donna facility as of March 31, 2021. (Fox News)
      
It’s one of a number of moves by the Biden administration to handle the influx. The Department of Health and Human Services announced earlier this month that it was opening an emergency intake site in Donna to house up to 1,500 teenagers near the Customs and Border Protection (CBP) center.
HHS OPENS NEW SITE FOR MIGRANT CHILDREN NEAR PACKED DONNA FACILITY
